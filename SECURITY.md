Security & Vulnerability Reporting
At [Platform Name], we take the security and integrity of our development platform very seriously. We believe that a secure ecosystem is built on transparency and collaboration with the security community.

If you discover a potential security vulnerability within [Platform Name], we encourage you to report it to us responsibly. We appreciate your efforts to help us keep our platform safe for all users.

How to Report a Vulnerability

1. Contact Information

Please send your report to:

Email: security@[platform-domain].com
PGP Key: [Link to your PGP key if you use encrypted email]
Alternative: [Link to a bug bounty platform like HackerOne or Bugcrowd, if applicable]

2. What to Include

To help us investigate and resolve the issue quickly, please include the following details in your report:

Description: A clear summary of the vulnerability and the specific impact.
Reproduction Steps: Detailed instructions on how to reproduce the issue (including URL, API endpoint, or specific code snippet).
Proof of Concept (PoC): If available, a script or screenshot demonstrating the vulnerability.
Environment: Browser version, OS, or specific platform configuration used.
Remediation Suggestions: Any ideas you have on how to fix the issue (optional).

3. What We Will Do

Acknowledgment: We will acknowledge receipt of your report within 48 hours.
Investigation: Our security team will analyze the report and determine its validity.
Communication: We will keep you updated on the progress of the investigation and the fix.
Resolution: Once fixed, we will notify you and provide credit if you wish to be named.
Responsible Disclosure Guidelines
We ask that you adhere to the following guidelines while investigating and reporting:

Do Not Exploit: Do not attempt to exploit the vulnerability beyond what is necessary to demonstrate the issue.
Do Not Disrupt: Do not disrupt our services or degrade the experience for other users (e.g., no Denial of Service attacks).
Do Not Access Unauthorized Data: Do not access, modify, or delete any data belonging to other users or the platform itself.
Do Not Publicize: Please do not disclose the vulnerability publicly (including social media, blogs, or public forums) until we have had a reasonable opportunity to fix it.

What We Consider Out of Scope
We are not looking for reports on the following, as they are considered outside the scope of our security program:

Issues requiring physical access to our servers or facilities.
Social engineering attacks (phishing, vishing) against our employees.
Issues related to third-party services we do not control (e.g., GitHub, AWS, Stripe).
Missing best practices (e.g., missing HTTP headers like X-Frame-Options) unless they lead to a direct exploit.
Denial of Service (DoS) attacks.
Our Commitment to You
No Retaliation: We will never take legal action against researchers who follow these guidelines.
Credit: If you wish, we will publicly credit you in our security advisories or blog posts once the issue is resolved.
Swag/Rewards: Qualifying reports may be eligible for monetary rewards or exclusive platform swag.
Thank you for helping us build a safer Internet for everyone.
